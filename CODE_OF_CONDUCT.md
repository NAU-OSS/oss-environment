# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge
We are committed to providing a welcoming, inclusive, and respectful environment for everyone who participates in this project—users, contributors, reviewers, and maintainers.

We expect good faith collaboration and professional conduct in all project spaces.

## Community Values & Principles
We aim to build a community that is:
- **Welcoming** to newcomers and people of all backgrounds
- **Respectful** in disagreement and discussion
- **Constructive** in feedback and technical debate
- **Collaborative** and focused on what’s best for the project
- **Safe** from harassment, intimidation, and abuse :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

## Expected Behavior
Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment include:
- Using welcoming and inclusive language
- Being respectful of differing viewpoints and experiences
- Giving and receiving constructive feedback gracefully
- Assuming good intent and asking questions before escalating conflict
- Focusing on issues, code, and ideas—not personal attacks
- Being mindful of tone in text-based communication :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

## Unacceptable Behavior
Examples of unacceptable behavior include:
- Harassment, threats, stalking, or sustained disruption
- Discrimination or derogatory comments (race, ethnicity, nationality, religion, gender identity, sexual orientation, disability, etc.)
- Personal insults, name-calling, or “dogpiling”
- Sexual language/images or unwelcome sexual attention
- Publishing someone’s private information (doxxing) without permission
- Trolling, deliberate provocation, or repeated bad-faith engagement
- Retaliation against anyone who reports a concern :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}

## Scope
This Code of Conduct applies within all project spaces (e.g., GitHub issues, pull requests, discussions, chat/Discord/Slack, and project events) and also applies when someone is representing the project in public spaces. :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}

## Enforcement Guidelines
Project maintainers are responsible for enforcing this Code of Conduct fairly and consistently.

When a violation is confirmed, maintainers may take any action they deem appropriate, including:
1. **Correction** – a private or public request to stop and change behavior
2. **Warning** – a clear statement that further violations will result in consequences
3. **Temporary Consequences** – temporary mute/lock, removal from threads, or temporary ban
4. **Permanent Consequences** – permanent ban from project spaces and/or removal of contributor privileges

Maintainers may also:
- Remove or edit content that violates this Code of Conduct
- Lock conversations that become unproductive or unsafe
- Escalate immediately to stronger action for severe incidents (e.g., threats, doxxing, harassment)

## Enforcement Responsibilities
Maintainers will:
- Treat reporters and targets of harm with respect
- Avoid victim-blaming and avoid “debating” someone’s lived experience
- Keep records of decisions when appropriate
- Prioritize community safety over winning arguments or preserving harmful participation :contentReference[oaicite:6]{index=6}
